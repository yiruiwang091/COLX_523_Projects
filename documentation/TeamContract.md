# Teamwork Contract

## Working Agreements 

### 1. Work Distribution

We will distribute work through open discussion at the beginning of each sprint. Tasks will be assigned based on each member's strengths, prior experience, and current workload. Our goal is to ensure both fairness and efficiency by aligning responsibilities with individual skill sets while still allowing everyone to contribute across different areas of the project.

### 2. Task Workflow and Coordination

After tasks are divided, we will organize our workflow in a logical sequence when necessary. If certain tasks depend on earlier components (e.g., data collection before annotation), the member responsible for the first stage will prioritize completing their portion early. This ensures that subsequent tasks can proceed smoothly without delays and keeps the project timeline on track.

### 3. Meeting Frequency

We will meet at least once per week. When possible, we will take advantage of time immediately after class to hold brief discussions or planning sessions. Additional meetings may be scheduled if a sprint requires closer coordination.

### 4. Scheduling and Meeting Records

All team meetings will be added to our shared calendars to ensure transparency and accountability. We will also record meeting times and brief summaries in a shared Google document to maintain documentation of decisions, task assignments, and progress updates.

### 5. Working Style

We will follow a structured sprint-based approach. At the start of each sprint, we will set clear internal deadlines for intermediate milestones rather than waiting until the final submission deadline. If necessary, we may schedule focused working sessions (e.g., short “hackathon-style” meetings) to complete critical tasks collaboratively.

### 6. Progress Updates

While we may not conduct formal daily stand-ups, we will provide regular written updates in Slack regarding task progress, especially if a task affects other members. Members are expected to communicate early if they encounter difficulties that may delay deliverables.

### 7. Availability Expectations

Team availability is flexible, as long as all members attend scheduled meetings and complete assigned work before internal deadlines. If a member anticipates scheduling conflicts, they will notify the team in advance so tasks can be adjusted accordingly.

### 8. Quality Expectations

Each member is responsible for producing high-quality work that is clear, organized, and logically sound. Code should be readable and documented where necessary. Written components should be proofread carefully for clarity, grammar, and consistency. We will collectively review major documents before submission.

### 9. Scrum Leadership

The role of Scrum leader will rotate weekly. The Scrum leader is responsible for facilitating meetings, ensuring task clarity, monitoring progress toward sprint deliverables, and confirming that work is distributed equitably across members.

### 10. Code Review Process

Each pull request must be reviewed by one other team member before merging into the main branch. We will follow a consistent review rotation order to ensure fairness and equal participation in code review responsibilities. Feedback should be constructive and focused on improving clarity, functionality, and structure.

### 11. Code of Conduct

We commit to maintaining a respectful and professional team environment. We will always listen to others' perspectives, communicate disagreements constructively, and treat all team members with respect. Unacceptable behavior includes dismissive communication, lack of accountability, or repeated failure to meet agreed-upon responsibilities without prior notice.
